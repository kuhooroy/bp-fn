Background:

The NYC Clean Heat Program is an intiative to make the city more energy efficient. It encourages and helps New York buildings to transition to cleaner heating methods, such as cold climate air source heat pumps, energy efficient ground source heat pumps, and heat pump water heaters. The key villains in our city's crisis are No. 4 and No. 6 fuels. Some of the city's supporting initiatives include the NYC DEP's Boiler Registration Program, which requires buildings to register any boilers that output more than 350,000 BTUs but less than 2.8 million BTUs, and the Greener Greater Buildings Plan, which is a comprehensive set of energy efficiency laws that targets New York Cityâ€™s largest existing buildings and requires them to do things like assess and report their energy and water usage every year and regulate the energy-efficiency of their designs. The Clean Heat Program and NYC are continuously providing resources and incentives to make the transition more accessible and efficient for everyone.
